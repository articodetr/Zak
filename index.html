Chapter 7 Bankruptcy

The fresh start — most unsecured debt wiped out in a matter of months.

Chapter 7 is the fastest and most common form of consumer debt relief. Qualifying unsecured debt is eliminated — discharged — in a matter of months, and thanks to Michigan and federal exemptions, the vast majority of filers keep everything they own. The moment your case is filed, federal law forces collection activity to stop.

Debts It Wipes Out

  • Credit cards & store cards
  • Medical & hospital bills
  • Personal loans & payday loans
  • Old utility, phone & rent balances
  • Repossession balances & most judgments

What You Keep

  • Your home & vehicle in most cases
  • Retirement accounts — 401(k), IRA, pensions
  • Household goods & personal belongings
  • Every dollar you earn after filing

Protection From Day One

  • Wage garnishments stop — often within days
  • Collector calls & letters end immediately
  • Lawsuits, levies & repossession frozen
  • Foreclosure activity paused

What It Can't Erase

  • Most student loans
  • Child support & alimony
  • Most recent income taxes
  • Court fines & criminal restitution
  • Timeline: about 3–5 months to discharge
  • Court: one short trustee meeting — usually no judge
  • Qualifying: income-based means test
  • Fees: flat, explained up front, payment plans available

Chapter 13 Bankruptcy

One affordable payment plan — keep your home, your car, and your peace of mind.

Chapter 13 reorganizes everything you owe into one court-protected monthly payment sized to your actual budget. The plan runs three to five years; when it ends, remaining qualifying balances are discharged. It's the tool of choice when you're behind on a home or car you intend to keep.

Built To Save Your Home

  • Stops foreclosure — even days before the sale
  • Missed mortgage payments spread across the plan
  • You keep making the regular payment going forward
  • Relief on some junior liens in qualifying cases

Protects Your Vehicle

  • Repossession stopped immediately
  • Missed car payments folded into the plan
  • Some loans restructured at a lower cost
  • Keep driving to work while you catch up

How The Plan Works

  • One monthly payment — no juggling creditors
  • Amount based on your real income & expenses
  • Runs 3–5 years, then qualifying debt is discharged
  • Can be adjusted if your circumstances change

Who It's Best For

  • Income above the Chapter 7 limits
  • Behind on a mortgage or car loan you want to keep
  • Protecting a co-signer on consumer debt
  • Property that exceeds exemption limits
  • Protection: automatic stay from the day of filing
  • Plan length: 3–5 years
  • Co-signers: shielded by the co-debtor stay
  • Fees: most payable through the plan itself

Wage Garnishment Defense

Up to 25% of your paycheck can be taken — we move fast to stop it.

Once a creditor wins a judgment, it can take up to 25% of every paycheck and freeze your bank accounts — and it won't stop until the debt, interest, and costs are paid in full. The law gives you a faster answer: the moment a bankruptcy case is filed, the automatic stay orders the garnishment to stop.

How We Stop It

  • Filing triggers the federal automatic stay
  • Your employer stops withholding — often within days
  • Bank account freezes lifted, new ones blocked
  • Works against nearly every judgment creditor

Money You May Recover

  • Wages taken in the 90 days before filing can sometimes be returned
  • Applies when the recent total tops $600
  • Every recent garnishment is reviewed
  • Recovered funds go back to you where the law allows

Beyond Bankruptcy

  • Objections to defective or improper garnishments
  • Exemption claims for protected income — Social Security, pensions, disability
  • Negotiation with creditors when filing isn't right for you
  • Defense against debt-buyer lawsuits before judgment

Why Acting Fast Matters

  • Every garnished check is money you likely won't get back
  • Interest & costs keep the balance growing
  • Other creditors can line up behind the first
  • Filing sooner protects more of your pay
  • Relief: often within days of filing
  • Exposure: up to 25% of each paycheck
  • Coverage: wages and bank accounts
  • First step: free, confidential case review

Debt Lawsuit Defense

A summons is how garnishments begin — answering it changes everything.

Nonstop calls and threatening letters often end with a court summons from a debt buyer you've never heard of. Ignoring it leads to a default judgment — and that's what opens the door to garnished wages and frozen accounts. Acting before judgment keeps every option open.

Before A Judgment

  • An answer is filed so no default is entered
  • Debt buyers are made to prove they own the debt
  • Negotiation from a position of strength
  • Bankruptcy evaluated if the numbers call for it

After A Judgment

  • Objections to defective or improper garnishments
  • Exemption claims for protected income — Social Security, pensions, disability
  • Filing bankruptcy stops collection the same day
  • Recent garnished wages reviewed for recovery
  • Deadline: court answers are due in days, not weeks
  • Exposure: up to 25% of each paycheck after judgment
  • First step: bring the summons to a free review
