Why Timing Matters

In Debt Trouble, Waiting Is The Most Expensive Choice

Most of the damage — seized wages, compounding interest, a foreclosure sale date — happens while people hope the problem will resolve itself. It won't. Here is what the law actually says.

01

Garnishment Won't Stop On Its Own

Once a creditor wins a judgment, up to 25% of every paycheck can be taken until the debt is paid in full — however long that takes.

02

Balances Grow While You Wait

Interest, late fees, and collection costs compound quietly, so the debt you face next month is always bigger than the one you face today.

03

Filing Protects You The Same Day

The moment your case is filed, the automatic stay stops garnishments, collector calls, lawsuits, and repossession — immediately.

01. What Types Of Cases Does Zak Handle?

The firm focuses on consumer debt relief: Chapter 7 bankruptcy, Chapter 13 bankruptcy, and wage garnishment defense — providing tailored legal solutions for each client across Metro Detroit and Michigan.

02 How Do I Schedule A Consultation?

Call 313-846-6400 any time — the firm is available 24/7 — or send a message through the contact form below. Your first consultation is always free.

03 What I Should Bring To My First Meeting?

A rough list of your debts, recent pay stubs, and any court or garnishment papers you've received. Don't worry if you're missing something — we'll help you gather it.

04 How Long Will My Case Take?

Most Chapter 7 cases discharge in about 3–5 months. Chapter 13 repayment plans run 3–5 years, but protections — like stopping garnishments and foreclosure — begin the day we file.

05 How Are Legal Fees Structured?

Your first consultation is always free. Fees are flat, explained up front, and flexible payment arrangements are available — no surprises.

06 Will I Lose My House Or My Car?

In the vast majority of cases, no. Michigan exemptions protect most homes, vehicles, and retirement accounts. Zak reviews exactly what's protected before anything is filed.
